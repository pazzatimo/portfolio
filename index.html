Who I Am

Practitioner. Innovator.
Community builder.

"The real power of data lies not in the numbers themselves, but in what happens when they reach the right hands at the right time."

Based in Morogoro, Tanzania, I am a versatile Data, M&E and ICT practitioner with over six years of hands on experience across health, governance, WASH and community development. I hold a Bachelor of Education in Information Communication Technology and carry multiple professional certifications in MEAL, Research Ethics, Project Management and Environmental Management.

I combine strong technical skills with real understanding of the community. I have designed and deployed MEAL systems for multi-partner programmes spanning 179 communities in three regions, trained over 1,000 students and 500+ youth on accountability and WASH, and mobilised awareness events reaching more than 10,000 community members. At the same time, I create responsive websites, write professional reports, and build ICT solutions for organisations, businesses, and individuals.

I coordinate easily with local government authorities, international NGOs and grassroots communities and I bring that same fluency to managing data, dashboards, toolkits and campaigns.

Reading & Reflection

Books that shaped me.
Words that stayed.

A curated shelf of books I recommend — and a wall of the quotes that live rent-free in my mind.

Recommended reading
📊
Thinking in Systems
Donella H. Meadows
The single most useful framework I have found for understanding why communities behave the way they do, why interventions fail, and what leverage actually means.
Data & M&E
📖
The Purpose Driven Life
Rick Warren
Read it first at twenty-two and it reoriented everything. Re-read it at thirty and it did the same thing, differently. The questions it asks do not expire.
Faith
🌍
Dead Aid
Dambisa Moyo
Controversial and necessary. Whether you agree with every argument or not, every person working in development in Africa should read this and feel uncomfortable — productively.
Development
💼
The Lean Startup
Eric Ries
The discipline of testing assumptions before building at scale applies to community programmes just as much as to tech products. A book that changed how I design M&E plans.
Business
🧠
Factfulness
Hans Rosling
A data-driven case for measured optimism. Rosling shows how our instincts about the world are systematically wrong — and gives us tools to think better. Required reading before any field assignment.
